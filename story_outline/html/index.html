Where does the money come from?

You may know you pay taxes, but don't how know those dollars get to schools, roads or the Mayor. The answer is funds. Cleveland has 8 funds, with each fund having restrictions on how the money can be spent and on what.


Funding for Judicials Services come from The General Fund. The General Fund covers expenses for what we typically think a city does - Mayor, city council, police and more.

More specifically, the General Fund covers expenses for 11 departments which cover the following areas:

  • Judicial
  • Courts
  • Public Safety
  • Fire
  • Emergency Responders
  • Waste Collection
  • Recreation
  • Health Centers
  • Housing
  • City Council
  • the Mayor’s office

The General Fund gets its money from many sources, but most of it (66%) comes from income taxes.

Where does the money go?

The Department of Public Safety makes up 56.26% of the General Fund budget. The next largest Department is the Department of Public Works, which only takes up 12.13%.

What reforms are being asked for today?

Here are the organizations working on police reform in Cleveland Black Sprint CLE Demands

1 Year

  • 15% reduction in total tax dollars spent on policing in Cuyahoga County
  • Public commitment from city officials in the 2021 election cycle to establish a pilot program to modify the dispatch system to direct non-police emergency services to non-violent 9-1-1 calls.
  • Establishment of a permanent, elected civilian review board over the Cleveland Police Department with firing power over all officers.
  • Construction of an East Side Trauma Center as paid for by the Cleveland Clinic and University's Hospitals
  • Public comment at all city-council meetings

3 Year
  • 25% Reduction in total tax dollars spent on policing in Cleveland
  • Abolition of at least 3 commissioned police departments in Cuyahoga County
  • Overhaul of dispatch methodologies to exclude police entirely from non-violent emergencies
  • Pilot program established to respond to mental health, domestic violence, or substance abuse related calls
  • Free city-wide WiFi
  • Development of a Resident Community Investment committee for the City of Cleveland overseen exclusively by Cleveland residents

5 Year
  • A single, accountable force under democractic control by the public is responsible for policing in Cuyahoga County.
  • 50% reduction in total tax dollars spent on policing in Cleveland
  • Decriminalize the possession or use of all drugs in Cuyahoga County, regardless of scheduled status
  • Expunge the records for drug-related possession charges in Cuyahoga County
    • ​​​​​​​
  • Solidification of pilot program into county safety budget to respond to mental health, domestic violence, and non-violent emergency calls. This codification shall not increase funding, but shift funding away from the Cleveland Police Department’s annual budget.
  • Non-gated healthcare for all Cleveland residents, including access to abortions and reproductive health care
  • Free Public Transit for all GCRTA buses and trains.
  • Unionized Green Jobs guarantee, regardless of citizenship status
  • Establishment of a Tax Abatement for Public Housing Development for at least 25,000 people.
